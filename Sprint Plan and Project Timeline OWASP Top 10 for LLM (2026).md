With Steve Wilson and Rock Lambros acting as co-leads, the project now moves from early input and direction-setting into structured execution.

The following plan outlines how we will move from community input to a finalized 2026 Top 10 list.

# **Pre-Sprint Phase**

## **Pre-Sprint 0: Team Formation and Setup**

**April 13 – April 20, 2026**

**Objective:** Set up the working structure for the project.

### **Activities**

* Assign **Entry Leads** for each of the existing Top 10 categories  
* Encourage community members to volunteer and participate  
* Directly interested contributors should connect with Rock Lambros to get involved  
* Distribute the updated markdown template for 2026 entries  
* Announce the process for submitting **new vulnerability proposals**  
* Share the full sprint plan with the community

### **Deliverables**

* Confirmed entry leads across all Top 10 categories  
* Contributors aligned to initial working groups  
* Templates and contribution model in circulation

# **Sprint Structure**

All sprints run **Monday to Monday** in two-week increments, beginning on **April 20, 2026**.

## **Sprint 1: Entry Definition**

**April 20 – May 4, 2026**

**Objective:** Refresh the existing Top 10 and generate new candidate entries.

### **Track A: Existing Entry Updates**

* Entry leads and sub-teams revise current Top 10 entries  
* Focus areas:  
  * Updated definitions  
  * Modern, relevant examples  
  * Stronger, actionable mitigations

### **Track B: New Entry Proposals**

* Open submission process for new vulnerabilities  
* Contributors use the standard markdown template  
* Submissions collected in a dedicated GitHub directory

### **Deliverables**

* Fully updated versions of the existing Top 10  
* A repository of proposed new entries

## **Sprint 2: Community Review and Voting**

**May 4 – May 18, 2026**

**Objective:** Gather structured community feedback.

### **Activities**

* Public comments across all entries  
* Voting on:  
  * Importance  
  * Clarity

### **Notes**

* Existing Top 10 entries and new proposals are evaluated **separately**  
* Feedback captured via GitHub and structured voting

### **Deliverables**

* Ranked feedback signals for both tracks

## **Sprint 3: Refinement and Candidate Selection**

**May 18 – June 1, 2026**

**Objective:** Incorporate feedback and converge on a candidate set.

### **Activities**

* Entry teams revise content based on feedback  
* Selection of **Top 5 new entries** based on:  
  * Voting  
  * Community discussion  
  * Observed industry relevance

### **Deliverables**

* 10 refined existing entries  
* 5 selected new candidates  
* **Total: 15 candidates**

## **Sprint 4: Final Voting and List Selection**

**June 1 – June 15, 2026**

### **Week 1: Final Voting**

* Single unified list of 15 candidates  
* Voting on:  
  * Importance  
  * Clarity  
* Supplemental analysis:  
  * Attempt mapping to known vulnerability sources (e.g., CVE), acknowledging current gaps in AI-specific coverage

### **Week 2: Finalization**

* Produce a **stack-ranked Top 10**  
* Editorial cleanup:  
  * Consistency  
  * Clarity  
  * Formatting

### **Deliverables**

* Final OWASP Top 10 for LLM Applications (2026)  
* Publication-ready draft

# **Key Milestone**

## **Final Handoff to Production**

**Target Date: June 15, 2026**

At this point, the list is:

* Finalized

* Ready for layout, design, and formal publication
